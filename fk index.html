INTRODUCING, NIGER STATE

THE POWER STATE

Experience the natural beauty and rich culture of Niger State, Nigeria's largest state. Known as the "Power State," it features stunning attractions like the Gurara Waterfalls and Kainji National Park. Immerse yourself in our diverse cultures, vibrant cities, and historic craftsmanship, especially in Bida, famous for its brass works and pottery. As a key energy hub with major hydroelectric dams, Niger State is both a cultural and economic cornerstone of Nigeria. Explore and enjoy the unique blend of adventure and tradition in Niger State. EMIRATE COUNCILS OF NIGER STATE

Borgu Emirate

The Borgu Emirate is located in the northern part of Niger State, bordering Benin Republic to the west. It has historical significance as part of the Borgu Kingdom, which was a prominent empire in the pre-colonial era.

Read More

Kagara Emirate

Kagara Emirate is situated in the central part of Niger State. It was established as part of the efforts to consolidate traditional governance structures within the region.

Read More

Lapai Emirate

The Lapai Emirate is located in the southern part of Niger State. It has a historical background as one of the traditional emirates in the region.

Gurara Waterfalls

A breathtaking natural wonder, perfect for picnics and photography, where cascading waters create a serene and picturesque landscape.

Zuma Rock

Often referred to as the "Gateway to Abuja," this iconic monolith is a symbol of strength and endurance, offering stunning views and a rich history to explore.
